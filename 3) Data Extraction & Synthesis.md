# Data Extraction & Synthesis

Once the relevant literature was collected, the next step involved data extraction and synthesis. Data extraction was performed narratively, meaning that the reviewer carefully read each article to identify and record relevant information. Key details, including study design, population characteristics, tACS dosage parameters, outcomes, and main findings, were systematically collected in a dedicated Excel spreadsheet to ensure organization and consistency. 

For this project, the primary focus was on change scores between baseline and post-intervention. Thus, the data extraction includes only the raw mean and SD for baseline and post-intervention scores. Scores reported for other timepoint measurements were not collected. 

To deal with any missing data, authors were contacted for the missing information. Given the limited tiemframe, if there was no response from an author within 2 weeks, the missing values were imputed from similar studies as recommended by the Cochrane Handbook. 

You can download the full data extraction table here: [Download the table](tACS_session_dose_response_Melody.xlsx).

*Please note, this file also contains additional information such as a study table of characteristics and an overview of cognitive outcomes. For the extracted data, please refer to the tabs "tACS MoCA", "tACS RAVLT", and "tACS ADAS-Cog".*

## Bias Assessment
After completing data extraction, a risk of bias assessment was conducted for each included study. The Cochrane ROB-2 tool was used, which employs structured signalling questions to evaluate potential sources of bias across several domains, including the randomization process, deviations from intended interventions, missing outcome data, measurement of outcomes, and selection of reported results. Assessments were performed separately according to study design, distinguishing between between-subject and within-subject studies, to ensure that each study was evaluated appropriately based on its methodological framework. 

- Within-subjects studies: [Download the table](ROB2_crossover.xlsm).
- Between-subjects studies: [Download the table](ROB2_parallel.xlsm).

Overall, the bias assessment indicated low risk for most of our studies. Although 1 or 2 studies showed a moderate risk in at least one domain, there was no one study with a high risk of bias.
